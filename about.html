About MultiOmics Inc.

Advancing earlier, more equitable breast cancer detection

MultiOmics Inc. develops LiquidMammo™, a microRNA‑based liquid biopsy designed to complement imaging in breast cancer screening.

Who we are

MultiOmics Inc. is a molecular diagnostics company focused on translating circulating microRNA biology into practical tools for earlier disease detection. The team spans molecular biology, biostatistics, clinical diagnostics, and population screening.

Our mission

We aim to improve breast cancer screening performance by complementing imaging with biologically‑driven signals — especially in populations where mammography alone underperforms.

Where we operate

MultiOmics Inc. is headquartered in Washington, DC, and operates out of LabCentral, Cambridge, MA — a premier innovation ecosystem associated with MIT and biotech venture creation.

The science behind LiquidMammo™

microRNA biology

LiquidMammo™ analyzes dysregulation patterns in circulating microRNAs — small, non‑coding RNA molecules associated with tumor biology — to detect early molecular changes that may precede imaging findings.

Multi‑marker panel

The proprietary panel integrates signals across multiple microRNA markers to increase robustness, reproducibility, and performance across heterogeneous patient groups.

Clinical rationale

By layering molecular data with imaging, clinicians can contextualize findings, reduce uncertainty, and support earlier action in women with dense breasts and those with equivocal imaging results.

Leadership & scientific advisors

Founding team

The founding team combines expertise in molecular diagnostics, clinical assay development, epidemiology, and biostatistics.

Clinical advisors

A network of breast imaging radiologists and oncology specialists inform assay design, clinical interpretation, and implementation strategy.

Academic collaborations

MultiOmics collaborates with leading academic clinicians to validate assay performance and evaluate real‑world utility.

Impact for patients

A non‑invasive tool that can help clarify equivocal imaging, reduce false positives, and support earlier detection in dense‑breast populations.

Impact for clinicians

Standardized, biologically‑based insights that integrate seamlessly into screening workflows without requiring additional procedures.

Business opportunity

Growing demand for liquid biopsy tools in early detection, payer interest in reducing long‑term screening costs, and a scalable CLIA LDT commercialization path.
