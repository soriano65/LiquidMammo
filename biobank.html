MultiOmics Biobank

The MultiOmics Biobank is a longitudinal breast‑cancer resource that enables rapid, retrospective discovery and validation of microRNA diagnostic panels for screening support. Its depth and historical span make it possible to test candidate signatures against known outcomes—accelerating iteration prior to prospective studies.

Scale & history

~20,000 breast‑cancer samples with longitudinal clinical context, including cohorts reaching back to 2008—enabling immediate retrospective performance assessment of candidate panels.

Consent & provenance

The DNA consent pathway was originally developed at Baylor; samples/data are held and managed by MultiOmics with consent‑based access controls.

What we bank

Specimens

Blood‑based materials suitable for microRNA profiling; where available, paired tissue and additional biospecimens are linked to support biology‑aware modeling. Pre‑analytical SOPs and QC criteria are shared on request.

Linked clinical data

Longitudinal context (e.g., diagnosis, subtype assignment, imaging/biopsy confirmation, and select time‑to‑event variables when available) supports rigorous label definition for training and validation. All datasets are de‑identified prior to release.

How investigators use the biobank

Discovery

Identify and refine candidate microRNA signatures associated with cancer presence and molecular subtype; optimize normalization, feature selection, and model calibration.

Retrospective validation

Quantify sensitivity, specificity, AUC, and calibration using historical samples with known outcomes to de‑risk prospective studies and subgroup analyses (e.g., density‑stratified).

Governance & participant protections

Consent & privacy

All access is consent‑driven and governed by data‑minimization and privacy safeguards. Only de‑identified data leave MultiOmics environments. Secondary use must align with the original consent scope.

Scientific & ethics review

Requests undergo documented review for scientific merit, feasibility, analytics plan, and ethical compliance. Where applicable, requesters provide IRB determination and a data‑security plan.

Accessing the biobank

Who can apply

Academic, clinical, or industry researchers with a focus on diagnostic discovery, validation, or clinical utility. Prioritization is given to projects that address early detection, dense‑breast limitations, or subtype‑aware decision pathways.

How to request

  1. Submit a 2–3 page concept with aims, endpoints, cohorts, and dataset needs.
  2. Provide IRB status (if applicable) and a brief data‑security plan.
  3. Agree to data‑use terms and attribution language.

Frequently asked

Do you share raw or processed data?

Both are supported depending on consent and review scope. For discovery, raw plus minimal metadata are common; for validation, processed matrices and analysis recipes may be approved to speed execution.

Turnaround time

Timelines depend on request complexity and review requirements. Well‑scoped validation requests are typically faster than complex, multi‑arm discovery studies.
