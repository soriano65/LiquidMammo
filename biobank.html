MultiOmics Biobank

Longitudinal samples to accelerate clinical validation

Our breast‑cancer biobank includes longitudinally profiled participants, enabling retrospective performance assessment of microRNA‑based screening panels without waiting for long prospective outcomes.

What makes this resource unique

Scale and history

The MultiOmics Biobank includes ~20,000 breast‑cancer samples with longitudinal data allowing immediate, retrospective validation of candidate signatures. This depth supports rapid iteration of diagnostic panels by linking molecular signals to known outcomes.

Consent & provenance

The DNA consent pathway was originally developed at Baylor, and ownership resides with MultiOmics. Consent and governance are structured to enable research while protecting participant rights and privacy.

What we bank

Specimens

The biobank supports liquid‑biopsy discovery and validation, focusing on blood‑based materials suitable for microRNA profiling. Where available, paired tissue and other clinical specimens are linked to support biology‑aware models.

Detailed specimen‑handling SOPs are shared under an approved access request and are updated periodically to reflect best practice in pre‑analytical quality control.

Linked clinical data

Longitudinal clinical context (e.g., diagnosis, subtype assignment, imaging/biopsy confirmation, and time‑to‑event variables when available) supports high‑fidelity label definition for training and validating diagnostic algorithms.

Variables and data dictionaries are provided on approval; all datasets are de‑identified prior to release.

How investigators use the biobank

Discovery

Identify candidate microRNA signatures associated with breast‑cancer presence and molecular subtype; optimize signal processing, normalization, and feature selection.

Retrospective validation

Quantify diagnostic performance (sensitivity, specificity, AUC) using historical samples with known outcomes to reduce risk ahead of prospective studies.

Governance & participant protections

Consent & privacy

All access is consent‑driven. Requests undergo a documented review that includes scientific merit, data‑minimization, and privacy safeguards. Only de‑identified data are released to external investigators.

Scientific & ethics review

External requests are evaluated for feasibility, analytic rigor, and alignment with participant consent. Results generated from biobank data are expected to be shared back for reproducibility.

Accessing the biobank

Who can apply

Academic, clinical, or industry researchers with a cancer‑diagnostics focus. We prioritize studies that improve early detection, address dense‑breast limitations, or clarify subtype‑specific pathways.

How to request

  1. Submit a short concept (2–3 pages) with aims, endpoints, and dataset needs.
  2. Provide IRB status (if applicable) and data‑security plan.
  3. Agree to data‑use terms and attribution language.
