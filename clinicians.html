Information for Clinicians

LiquidMammo™ is a supplemental, non‑invasive microRNA‑based liquid biopsy designed to strengthen breast‑cancer screening performance — especially in dense breasts where mammographic sensitivity is reduced.

LiquidMammo is currently being developed as a CLIA Laboratory Developed Test (LDT). It is not yet FDA‑cleared/approved and is undergoing analytical and clinical validation.

Why consider a supplemental molecular test?

Improved assessment in dense breasts

Mammographic sensitivity may fall to ~61% in BI‑RADS D density. A density‑independent molecular signal can improve overall diagnostic confidence.

Non‑invasive & easy to add

A routine blood draw — no radiation, no compression, minimal patient burden.

Supports clinical decision‑making

Provides complementary biological information to guide follow‑up strategies and escalation to diagnostic imaging where appropriate.

Biological rationale

microRNAs regulate gene expression across oncogenic pathways, including proliferation, immune modulation, and metastatic signaling. Their stability in blood and early dysregulation during tumorigenesis make them promising biomarkers for screening support.

How the test works

1 — Order through your practice

The test is clinician‑ordered like any routine lab assay. Phlebotomy can be performed in‑clinic or via standard partner labs.

2 — Laboratory microRNA analysis

Plasma is stabilized and processed under standardized SOPs. A multi‑marker microRNA signature is quantified and analyzed.

3 — Clinician‑ready report

You receive a structured report including result classification, density‑contextual interpretation, and recommendations for appropriate follow‑up.

Ideal patient populations

  • Women with dense breasts (BI‑RADS C or D).
  • Patients with elevated genetic or family‑history risk.
  • Patients who defer mammography due to pain or discomfort.
  • Younger women with risk factors who are not yet eligible for routine mammography.
  • Patients requiring more frequent monitoring between imaging intervals.

Interpreting results

Negative / Low‑risk

Suggests no detectable microRNA signature consistent with cancer‑associated patterns. Continue guideline‑directed screening.

Positive / Elevated signal

Indicates presence of microRNA changes associated with tumor biology. Consider diagnostic mammography, ultrasound, or MRI where clinically appropriate.

Interpretation should always consider clinical history, density, and imaging context.

Safety, quality, and compliance

CLIA environment

Testing is performed in a certified CLIA laboratory under strict SOPs, QC checkpoints, and proficiency testing.

Sample integrity

Stabilized plasma handling ensures reproducibility. Barcoding and chain‑of‑custody maintain sample traceability.

Privacy & security

All patient data are de‑identified and processed under HIPAA‑aligned data‑handling workflows.

Clinician enrollment

We are partnering with select practices for early access and validation‑phase collaboration. Clinics serving dense‑breasted and under‑screened populations are especially encouraged to inquire.
