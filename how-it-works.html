How LiquidMammo™ Works

LiquidMammo™ is being developed as a microRNA-based liquid biopsy that provides a biological signal of breast cancer risk to complement mammography—especially in dense-breasted individuals where imaging sensitivity is reduced.

LiquidMammo is currently in development as a CLIA Laboratory Developed Test (LDT) and is not yet FDA‑cleared/approved.

Why a blood‑based test?

Density-independent

microRNA signals are not affected by tissue density, eliminating one of the biggest limitations of mammography for many women.

Early biological changes

microRNAs involved in cancer pathways can shift before structural changes become visible on imaging.

Simple & non-invasive

Only a blood draw—no radiation, no compression, no specialized scheduling.

The process

LiquidMammo is designed to integrate easily into routine clinical workflows. Here’s how the process works:

1 — Order placed by clinician

A clinician orders the test during a routine visit. No fasting or preparation is required. Phlebotomy can occur in‑clinic or via standard partner labs.

2 — Blood draw & microRNA stabilization

A small blood sample is collected and stabilized via standard laboratory tubes to preserve microRNA integrity for analysis.

3 — Laboratory analysis

Plasma is processed under controlled SOPs. A multi-marker microRNA signature associated with breast cancer pathways is quantified and analyzed.

4 — Actionable report to clinician

Your clinician receives a structured result with interpretation and recommended next steps, such as routine screening or diagnostic follow‑up if indicated.

The science inside the test

microRNA dysregulation

Breast tumors release or alter levels of specific microRNAs involved in oncogenic pathways (proliferation, apoptosis, immune evasion).

Signal detected in blood

These circulating microRNAs can be measured in plasma, generating a molecular pattern that distinguishes cancer from non‑cancer states.

Multi‑marker signature

LiquidMammo uses a combination of markers—not a single microRNA—to improve sensitivity, specificity, and robustness across populations.

Intended use

LiquidMammo is being designed as a supplemental screening tool to help clinicians determine when additional imaging or closer monitoring may be appropriate. It is not intended to replace mammography or clinical breast exams.
