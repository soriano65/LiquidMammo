Breast cancer screening, reimagined.

A microRNA blood test designed to complement imaging.

Early molecular changes often precede structural findings on mammography. LiquidMammo provides a non‑invasive signal that strengthens clinical decision‑making—especially where breast density limits traditional imaging performance.

Join Early Access Clinician Enrollment

Regulatory: LiquidMammo is being developed as a CLIA Laboratory Developed Test (LDT); not yet FDA‑cleared/approved.

Dense breasts challenge mammography

Sensitivity drops to ~61% in extremely dense tissue. Interval cancers are more common. A molecular complement may reduce misses.

Earlier biological signal

microRNA shifts can occur before imaging-visible lesions. Combining biology + imaging strengthens screening performance.

Addresses real-world variability

Mammographic detection varies by density, age, equipment, and reader. A standardized molecular layer improves consistency.

Clinical context

  • 1 in 8 cancers missed by mammography overall
  • Higher interval cancer rates in dense breasts
  • ~50–60% false‑positive probability over 10 years
  • ~61% sensitivity in extremely dense tissue
  • 2–3 per 1,000 cancers detected in younger women
  • Lower participation when imaging alone is used

How it works

1 — Ordered by clinician

Clinician‑initiated blood draw at routine visit; no preparation needed.

2 — microRNA profiling

A proprietary multi‑marker panel quantifies tumor‑associated microRNA dysregulation.

3 — Actionable report

Results delivered to clinicians with density‑aware interpretation and subgroup context.
