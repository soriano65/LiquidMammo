Non‑invasive • Clinician‑ordered • Density‑aware

Breast cancer screening, reimagined.

A microRNA blood test designed to complement imaging.

Early molecular changes often precede structural findings on mammography. LiquidMammo provides a non‑invasive signal that strengthens clinical decision‑making—especially where breast density limits traditional imaging performance.

Join Early Access Clinician Enrollment

Regulatory: LiquidMammo is being developed as a CLIA Laboratory Developed Test (LDT); not yet FDA‑cleared/approved.

Dense breasts challenge mammography

Sensitivity drops to ~61% in extremely dense tissue. Interval cancers are more common. A molecular complement may reduce misses.

Earlier biological signal

microRNA shifts can occur before imaging-visible lesions. Combining biology + imaging strengthens screening performance.

Addresses real‑world variability

Mammographic detection varies by density, age, equipment, and reader. A standardized molecular layer improves consistency.

Clinical context

  • 1 in 8 cancers missed by mammography overall
  • Higher interval cancer rates in dense breasts
  • ~50–60% false‑positive probability over 10 years
  • ~61% sensitivity in extremely dense tissue
  • 2–3 per 1,000 cancers detected in younger women
  • Lower participation when imaging alone is used

LiquidMammo is designed to complement—not replace—imaging. By layering molecular signals with mammography, clinicians can contextualize risk, reduce uncertainty, and improve consistency across subgroups.

How it works

1 — Ordered by clinician

Clinician‑initiated blood draw at routine visit; no preparation needed.

2 — microRNA profiling

A proprietary multi‑marker panel quantifies tumor‑associated microRNA dysregulation.

3 — Actionable report

Results delivered to clinicians with density‑aware interpretation and subgroup context.

About MultiOmics Inc.

Translating molecular signals into earlier, more equitable detection

Who we are

MultiOmics Inc. develops LiquidMammo™, a microRNA‑based liquid biopsy designed to complement imaging in breast cancer screening. We are headquartered in Washington, DC, and operate out of LabCentral, Cambridge, MA (MIT innovation ecosystem).

Scientific foundation

Our approach leverages multi‑marker microRNA dysregulation associated with tumor biology. By combining molecular and imaging signals, LiquidMammo aims to strengthen clinical decision‑making—especially in populations where breast density reduces mammographic sensitivity.

Team & advisors

Founding team with backgrounds in molecular diagnostics, oncology, and biostatistics; clinical advisors if approved for public use. --> We collaborate with leading academic clinicians to refine assay performance and evaluate clinical utility.

Impact for patients

A non‑invasive blood test can reduce anxiety from equivocal imaging, broaden access for individuals who defer imaging, and support earlier detection in dense breasts—without adding procedural burden.

Business & access

LiquidMammo is being advanced as a CLIA LDT with a focused early‑access rollout. The model emphasizes clinician adoption, payer engagement, and partnerships that support equitable screening access.
