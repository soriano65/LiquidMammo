Breast cancer screening, reimagined.

A microRNA blood test designed to complement imaging—non‑invasive and clinician‑ordered.

Screening mammography is valuable but not perfect; false‑negatives occur and are more likely in dense breasts. Many screening programs also experience frequent false‑positives.

Join Early Access Clinician Enrollment

Regulatory: LiquidMammo is being developed as a CLIA Laboratory Developed Test (LDT); it is not yet FDA‑cleared/approved. Use is at the ordering clinician’s discretion within applicable regulations.

Why a blood‑based screen matters

  • False‑negative mammograms occur (≈1 in 8 overall).
  • False‑positives are common over a decade of screening.
  • Missed cancers (interval cancers) are more common in dense breasts.

What LiquidMammo does

Screening support A blood test that raises or lowers concern so clinicians can fast‑track imaging when warranted.

Actionable biology If elevated, the report maps results to the three clinical groups—HR+/HER2‑, HER2+, and triple‑negative—to guide the next test. (Imaging/biopsy confirm.)

Clinically motivated

Evidence shows mammography can miss cancers—particularly in dense tissue—and false‑negative rates have risen in large U.S. datasets.

A non‑invasive test may improve participation and complement imaging pathways.

Built for clinical workflows

Designed for density limitations

Dense breast tissue can obscure cancers on mammograms and is associated with higher interval cancer rates; adjunct methods can help address this gap.

50–74% of women in their 40s have dense breasts, and mammography sensitivity drops to ≈61% in extremely dense tissue (BCSC data). See the data →

Grounded in published evidence

We reference and align with publicly available data on screening performance (false‑negatives, false‑positives, detection rates per 1,000).
