Breast cancer screening reimagined

A supplemental microRNA-based blood test designed to complement breast imaging by providing biologically-driven insight—especially where breast density limits mammographic performance.

LiquidMammo is being developed as a CLIA Laboratory Developed Test (LDT); not yet FDA‑cleared or FDA‑approved.

Dense breasts challenge mammography

Sensitivity can fall to ~61% in extremely dense tissue. A molecular complement reduces misses.

Earlier biological signal

microRNA dysregulation can appear before imaging-visible changes.

Addresses real-world variability

Imaging performance varies with density, age, equipment, and technologist. A biological layer improves consistency.

Clinical context

  • Mammograms can miss cancers, especially in dense breast tissue.
  • Interval cancers—those found between screenings—are more common in women with dense breasts.
  • False positives are frequent: many women experience extra imaging or follow‑ups over 10 years.
  • In extremely dense tissue, mammography sensitivity can drop to ~61%.
  • Younger women often have denser breasts, making cancers harder to detect on imaging alone.
  • Screening adherence decreases when imaging feels uncertain or stressful.

LiquidMammo is designed to support—not replace—imaging by adding a biological signal that complements mammograms. Combining molecular insight with imaging helps strengthen early detection.

How it works

1 — Ordered by clinician

Blood draw at routine visit; no preparation required.

2 — microRNA profiling

A multi‑marker panel quantifies tumor‑associated biological signals.

3 — Clinician‑ready report

Delivered with density‑aware interpretation and subgroup context.
