Breast cancer screening reimagined

A supplemental microRNA-based blood test designed to complement breast imaging by providing biologically‑driven insight — particularly where dense tissue makes cancers harder to detect.

LiquidMammo is being developed as a CLIA Laboratory Developed Test (LDT); not yet FDA‑cleared or FDA‑approved.

Dense breasts challenge mammography

Dense tissue can hide cancers on imaging, reducing sensitivity and increasing uncertainty.

Earlier biological signal

microRNA changes can emerge before tumors become visible on mammograms.

More consistent screening

A biological complement helps add confidence and consistency across diverse populations.

Clinical context

  • Mammograms can miss cancers, especially in dense breast tissue.
  • Interval cancers—those found between screenings—occur more often in dense breasts.
  • False positives are common, leading many women to undergo additional imaging over time.
  • In extremely dense breasts, imaging sensitivity may fall to ~61%.
  • Younger women typically have denser breasts, making detection more difficult.
  • Screening adherence drops when imaging alone feels uncertain or stressful.

LiquidMammo is designed to support—not replace—imaging. By combining molecular insight with mammography, clinicians can strengthen early detection for a wider range of patients.

How it works

1 — Ordered by clinician

A simple blood draw performed during a routine visit.

2 — microRNA profiling

A multi‑marker microRNA panel is analyzed to detect biological signals.

3 — Clinician‑ready report

Results are delivered with density‑aware interpretation and screening guidance.
