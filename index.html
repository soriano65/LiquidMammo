A supplemental microRNA-based blood test that adds biological insight to mammography — particularly helpful in dense breast tissue, where imaging may miss early signs.

Dense breasts challenge mammography

Dense tissue can hide cancers on imaging, reducing sensitivity and increasing uncertainty.

Earlier biological signal

microRNA changes may appear before tumors become visible on imaging.

More consistent screening

A biological complement helps improve confidence across diverse patient groups.

Clinical context

  • Mammograms have limitations, especially in dense breasts.
  • Dense tissue can obscure early cancers on imaging.
  • False positives are common and may lead to follow‑up imaging.
  • In very dense tissue, mammogram sensitivity can fall to ~61%.
  • Younger women tend to have denser breasts, making detection harder.
  • Screening performance varies with density, age, and imaging equipment.

LiquidMammo is designed to support—not replace—mammography. Adding a biological signal gives clinicians an additional layer of insight to strengthen early detection.

How it works

1 — Ordered by clinician

A routine blood draw performed at a clinic or partner site.

2 — microRNA profiling

A multi‑marker microRNA panel is analyzed for meaningful biological patterns.

3 — Clinician‑ready report

Results include density‑aware interpretation and screening guidance.
