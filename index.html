Breast cancer screening reimagined

A supplemental microRNA-based blood test designed to complement breast imaging by providing biologically‑driven insight — especially where dense tissue makes cancers harder to detect.

Dense breasts challenge mammography

Dense tissue can hide cancers on imaging, reducing sensitivity and increasing uncertainty.

Earlier biological signal

microRNA changes can appear before tumors become visible on mammography.

More consistent screening

A biological complement adds confidence and consistency across diverse populations.

Clinical context

  • Mammograms can miss cancers, especially in dense breasts.
  • Interval cancers appear more often in women with dense tissue.
  • False positives are common and can lead to repeat imaging.
  • In extremely dense tissue, sensitivity may fall to ~61%.
  • Younger women are more likely to have dense breasts.
  • Adherence drops when imaging alone feels uncertain.

LiquidMammo is designed to support—not replace—imaging by adding a biological signal. Combining molecular insight with imaging helps strengthen early detection.

How it works

1 — Ordered by clinician

A routine blood draw performed during a visit.

2 — microRNA profiling

A multi‑marker panel is analyzed for tumor‑associated patterns.

3 — Clinician‑ready report

Results include density‑aware interpretation and guidance.
