Privacy & Compliance

At MultiOmics Inc., we are committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring that all biological samples and personal information are handled securely, ethically, and transparently. LiquidMammo™ is being developed under CLIA Laboratory Developed Test (LDT) guidelines, and all data practices follow strict laboratory and privacy standards.

LiquidMammo is not yet FDA‑cleared or FDA‑approved. All testing is performed under CLIA regulations.

What information we collect

  • Personal contact information (name, email, phone) when you or your clinician request the test.
  • Clinical information provided by your clinician to assist with test interpretation.
  • Biological samples (blood) collected for microRNA analysis.
  • Technical metadata used to support laboratory operations and quality control.

How your sample is used

Your blood sample is used solely to perform the LiquidMammo microRNA analysis. After analysis:

  • Samples are processed in a secured, certified CLIA laboratory.
  • Samples may be retained temporarily for quality assurance and regulatory compliance.
  • Samples are never sold, transferred, or shared outside approved laboratory workflows.
  • Samples may be de‑identified for internal validation research if permitted under your consent.

How we protect your data

De‑identification

All patient information used for analysis and validation is de‑identified when possible and separated from personal identifiers.

Secure storage

Data are stored on encrypted systems with restricted access, audit logging, and role‑based permissions.

HIPAA-aligned workflows

While LDTs are not always covered entities, we align our data-handling practices with HIPAA privacy and security principles.

When we share data

We do not sell, trade, or share your information with advertisers, third‑party marketers, or external organizations not directly involved in laboratory operations.

Your data may be shared only with:

  • Your ordering clinician and authorized care team.
  • Accredited laboratory personnel performing the test.
  • Regulatory bodies or auditors when required by law.

Your rights

  • The right to request how your data are used or stored.
  • The right to obtain a copy of your test result from your clinician.
  • The right to withdraw consent (depending on test status and sample processing stage).
  • The right to request deletion of personal data when legally possible.

Consent

Before your clinician orders LiquidMammo, you may be asked to sign a consent form that explains the purpose of testing, risks, benefits, how your sample will be used, and your data privacy rights.
